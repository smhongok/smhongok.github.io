Seongmin Hong

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Institute of New Media and Communications (INMC) at Seoul National University (SNU), currently completing alternative military service.

I earned my Ph.D. from the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Seoul National University, advised by Prof. Se Young Chun (ICL). I view generative AI not as a one-shot sampler, but as a differentiable optimization process, and my research focuses on endowing modern generative architectures with invertibility and differentiability. This perspective enables physically grounded inference in imaging and integrity-by-design generation for public-facing media. My doctoral thesis was awarded the Distinguished Ph.D. Dissertation Award.

Exhibitions & Art Projects

Engineer's AI Media Art: Digital Assemblage

Seoul Science Museum  ·  February 2026

Led a special public workshop at Seoul Science Museum demonstrating DiffBMP technology to create digital assemblage artworks. Taught participants how to transform everyday objects into artistic expressions using simple recognition methods, exploring the intersection of engineering, technology, and art.

IPA (Interactive Perspective Art): Ciao, Technology

SNU Powerplant  ·  January 2026

Collaborative kinetic art exhibition at SNU Powerplant. Served as lead for software infrastructure and typography for the signature piece Ordered Volume—a robotic sculpture utilizing 160 motors and spherical voxels to translate digital data into physical forms.

ARRCO: Multi-view 3D Crystal Point-Cloud Art

Independent crowd-funded project  ·  2025

Developed the full computational pipeline for high-density point-cloud laser engraving to create multi-view optical illusions where distinct images emerge from different observer perspectives.
